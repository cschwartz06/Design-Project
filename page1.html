Cookies:


When baking any type of cookie, it is important to use room temperature butter, as well as not overmixing the dough, because the cookies can become tough. Something that is optional but you can do to reduce cookies from spreading too much, is chilling the dough in the fridge for a bit before putting them in the oven. Using molasses in your chocolate chip cookies makes them more flavourful and overall better. Adding more brown sugar than white suagr to your recipe usually helps create softer cookies. For crispier cookies, you shoud use more white sugar than brown sugar. It is important to line the pan you are baking the cookies on with parchment paper to prevent the cookies from sticking to the pan. Lastly, make sure to let the cookies cool down before moving them.

Cake:

When baking cakes, it is best to use room temperature ingredients. Make sure to not overmix your cake batter to prevent the cake from being too dense. Greasing your cake pans before putting them in the oven helps prevent the cake from sticking to the pan, and tapping the pan on the counter helps remove unwanted air bubbles. To test if the cake is ready, use a toothpick to test it, if it comes out clean, then the cake is ready. Make sure the cake is fully cooled before decorating.

Muffins:

When baking muffins, it is extremely important to not overmix the batter, or the muffins may become too dense. When filling the muffin pan, fill each one to about 3/4 of the way. Like baking a cake, you can test if your muffins are ready with a toothpick. Make sure to add your ingredients like chocolate chips or blueberries at the end to avoid crushing them.
